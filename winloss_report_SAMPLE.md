# Win/Loss Intelligence Report

## Executive Summary

Out of 40 lost deals analyzed, two distinct loss patterns account for the majority of churned revenue opportunities. We are losing small, fast-moving deals to Competitor A on implementation speed, and losing large enterprise deals to Competitor B on price. These are two different problems requiring two different fixes - neither is solved by "be more competitive" in general.

## Pattern 1: We lose small Healthcare deals to Competitor A because we're too slow to onboard

**The numbers:** Every single deal lost to Competitor A (14 out of 14) was lost specifically because of implementation speed - not price, not features. These losses are concentrated in deals under ₹10 lakh (14 of 15 losses in that size band cite implementation speed) and in the Healthcare industry (14 of 18 Healthcare losses, ~78%).

**Why this is likely happening:** Our standard implementation process appears to be built for larger, more complex deployments. Smaller Healthcare customers - often clinics or small practices with simpler needs and real urgency (e.g., compliance deadlines) - don't need our full setup process, but they experience the same multi-week timeline as a large enterprise customer would.

**Suggested action:** Create a "fast-track" implementation tier for deals under ₹10 lakh with a target go-live of under 2 weeks, even if it means a more templated, less customized setup. Pilot this specifically with Healthcare prospects first, since that's where the pattern is sharpest.

## Pattern 2: We lose large enterprise deals to Competitor B purely on price

**The numbers:** 100% of losses to Competitor B (12 of 12) were attributed to pricing, and these losses are concentrated in deals over ₹30 lakh (12 of 21 large-deal losses, ~57%).

**Why this is likely happening:** Competitor B appears to have a more aggressive enterprise discounting strategy, and our pricing floor at this deal size isn't flexible enough for procurement-led large-account negotiations.

**Suggested action:** Introduce a formal enterprise discount band (e.g., a pre-approved discount range for deals over ₹30 lakh) so reps don't lose deals waiting on ad-hoc approval, or losing them outright because the floor wasn't competitive. Pair this with tracking Competitor B's typical discount level on deals where they're known to be in the running.

## Pattern 3: Small-to-mid deals show a secondary "other" loss cluster worth a closer look

**The numbers:** In the ₹10L-₹30L deal band, 3 of 4 losses (75%) fall into the "other" category - notably higher than the 12.5% baseline rate for "other" losses overall.

**Why this is likely happening:** This sample is small (4 deals), so it may be noise, but it's worth flagging because "other" often hides reasons that don't fit existing tags - things like deals going cold with no clear reason given.

**Suggested action:** For the next quarter, require reps to select a more specific reason when closing deals as lost in this size band, rather than defaulting to a generic note - this will turn "other" into something analyzable next time.
